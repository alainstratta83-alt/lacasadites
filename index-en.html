The Strategic Location

Donnas: Gateway to the Aosta Valley

At 322 meters altitude, Donnas is the natural entrance to the Aosta Valley. La Casa di Tes is located on the hill of Donnas at 550 m altitude, enjoying a mild climate all year round and spectacular views.

Mild Climate

Pleasant temperatures in all seasons thanks to the strategic position

Fort of Bard - A Few Minutes Away

An imposing fortress just a few minutes from La Casa di Tes, Fort of Bard is one of the most spectacular monuments in the Alps. It hosts museums, exhibitions and cultural events all year round.

Donnas: A Village to Discover

Unique Historical Heritage

The Arch and Roman Road of Gaul

The magnificent Roman Arch and the ancient Via delle Gallie (1st century BC) are extraordinary testimonies of the passage of history. A millennia-old heritage that makes Donnas unique in the Alps.

The Castles of Aosta Valley

A journey through medieval towers, fortresses and royal residences in the heart of the Alps

The Aosta Valley is home to over 70 castles and fortifications, testimony to a glorious past. Each manor tells stories of knights, noble families and epic battles, set in breathtaking Alpine landscapes.

FÃ©nis Castle

The most famous castle in the Aosta Valley, with its battlemented towers and fifteenth-century frescoes. A masterpiece of perfectly preserved medieval architecture.

Savoy Castle - Gressoney

The summer residence of Queen Margherita of Savoy, a jewel in eclectic style surrounded by the peaks of Monte Rosa. A fairytale castle in the heart of the Alps.

Savoy Castle Panoramic

Panoramic view of Savoy Castle surrounded by Alpine nature. Its five towers stand out against the sky of the mountains of the Aosta Valley.

Issogne Castle

Elegant Renaissance residence with the famous porticoed courtyard and the "Fountain of the Pomegranate". A dive into court life in the fifteenth century.

VerrÃ¨s Castle

Imposing military fortress with a square plan, unique example of fourteenth-century monoblock architecture. Majestically dominates the valley below.

Fort of Bard

Spectacular nineteenth-century military fortress that resisted Napoleon's siege. Today it houses museums and modern art exhibitions.

Sarriod de la Tour Castle

Elegant medieval castle in Saint-Pierre, famous for the "Room of Heads" with 171 carved corbels. A treasure of Aosta Valley medieval art.
